About Me

I am a Ph.D. student in the Department of Mathematics (Financial Mathematics Track) at Florida State University, advised by Dr. Ali Devran Kara. I am concurrently completing an M.Sc. in Mathematics (Financial Mathematics), expected Spring 2026, CGPA 3.7/4.0.

My research lies at the intersection of stochastic optimal control, reinforcement learning theory, mean-field games and control, and computational probability. A central theme is developing tractable approximation schemes for control problems driven by temporally correlated, non-i.i.d. noise — using belief-state augmentation and Dobrushin contraction arguments to establish near-optimality guarantees for finite-memory truncated policies.

I received my B.Sc. in Applied Mathematics from the University of Dhaka, Bangladesh (CGPA: 3.5/4.0, 2019–2023). Outside academia I enjoy chess, science fiction cinema, and anime.

Research

My dissertation develops theoretically grounded approximation schemes for stochastic control problems driven by long-memory, temporally correlated ("off-white") noise. The central framework uses belief-state augmentation, filter stability, finite-memory truncations, and Dobrushin contraction coefficients to yield near-optimality bounds for computationally tractable policies.
